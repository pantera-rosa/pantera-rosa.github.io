Data Overview

The data provided through this competition consists of two main categories: flight information and weather information, distributed throughout 200GB of 4 different data sources (FUSER, METAR, TAF, and CWAM). This data ranges from September 2022 to September 2023, with the training + validation datasets consisting of 24 consecutive days per month and the test dataset consisting of 8 days per month. There are 4 hour gaps in the test dataset, which is where we make our predictions.

Features of Interest

There are certain features that stood out to us given their known relevance to flight maneuvers:

  • Wind Speed, Wind Gusts, and Wind Direction

Aircraft take off and land into the wind whenever possible for better lift and control. Headwinds slow down the aircraft, increasing fuel consumption and extending flight times. Tailwinds shorten flight times and save fuel. Sudden, strong changes in wind speed (called wind gusts) can cause turbulence during takeoff, landing, and even mid-flight. They may require pilots to adjust flight paths or speeds. Changes in wind direction may force air traffic controllers to switch active runways, causing delays. High wind gusts can complicate landing approaches, especially in crosswind conditions. Airports have specific crosswind limits for safe operations, and gusts exceeding these limits may result in diversions or delays.

  • Precipitation and Thunderstorms

Thunderstorms are hazardous due to turbulence, lightning, hail, and strong updrafts or downdrafts. Flights may need to reroute around storm systems, causing delays. Airports might temporarily close during severe thunderstorms due to lightning risks for ground crews.

  • Cloud Cover

Low cloud cover reduces visibility during takeoff and landing, requiring Instrument Landing Systems (ILS) and additional pilot training. Dense cloud cover can complicate approaches and departures, potentially leading to delays or diversions to airports with better visibility.

  • Airport Code

An airport's location (i.e. KJFK -> K for the United States, JFK for John F Kennedy in New York City) is likely to be correlated with some changes in flight time, either due to the weather surrounding the aiport, the personnel maintaining the airport, or a number of other factors.
