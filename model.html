Model Design

Preexisting Research

There have been previous attempts at predicting thoroughput via machine learning. One study, Prediction of Hourly Airport Operational Throughput with a Multi-Branch Convolutional Neural Network, utilized convective weather information from the NOAA and flight trajectory data to train a multi-branch convolutional neural network (MB-ResNet) to predict flight arrivals at KATL. This paper explicitly mentions the importance of their weather tensor, convection indicators, traffic flow data, and nominal path indicators, leading us to believe that these features are likely to be relevant in our approach as well. With a RMSE of 0.11 in predicting arrivals and departures, this model demonstrates strong performance.
