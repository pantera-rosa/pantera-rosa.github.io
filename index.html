FlightHunters

The NASA Airport Thoroughput Prediction Challenge

Ilona Bodnar, Carol Sanchez Garibay, Fan Lin

Background

During peak periods, the Federal Aviation Administration (FAA) monitors and directs over 5,000 flights simultaneously. Every day, FAA's Air Traffic Organization (ATO) provides service to more than 45,000 flights and 2.9 million airline passengers across more than 29 million square miles of airspace. With over 10,000,000 scheduled passenger flights yearly, it is crucial that we understand all the factors that impact flight time and flight arrivals.

The Challenge

This challenge aims to develop a regression model capable of accurately forecasting the number of flight arrivals (throughput) at U.S. airports within a short-term timeframe. Specifically, the task is to predict, given an airport ABC and a timestamp TT, the expected number of arriving flights within the next three hours, segmented into 15-minute intervals.

Accurate throughput predictions are essential for optimizing the utilization of airport resources, runways, and the broader National Airspace System (NAS). This enables air traffic management systems to better handle the constraints imposed by limited capacity, especially during periods of high demand or adverse weather conditions.

Preexisting Research

Previous research has explored the use of machine learning to predict airport throughput. One notable study, Prediction of Hourly Airport Operational Throughput with a Multi-Branch Convolutional Neural Network, utilized convective weather data from the NOAA alongside flight trajectory information to train a multi-branch convolutional neural network (MB-ResNet) for forecasting flight arrivals at Hartsfieldâ€“Jackson Atlanta International Airport (KATL). The study emphasized the importance of incorporating weather tensors, convection indicators, traffic flow data, and nominal path predictors as critical features. These findings suggest that such factors are likely to play a significant role in improving the performance of throughput prediction models, providing a strong foundation for further exploration in this domain.
